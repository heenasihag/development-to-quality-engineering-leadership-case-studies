# Case Study 1: From Software Development to Automation Leadership

## Context
My career began in software development roles across banking and insurance systems, where I worked on coding, design participation, JCL/PROC creation, impact analysis, production issue resolution, and batch support.

## Challenge
As systems became more integration-heavy and release cycles demanded better speed and confidence, validation needed to move beyond manual or siloed approaches.

## What I brought forward from development
- system-level understanding
- strong debugging mindset
- comfort with backend and batch workflows
- ability to analyze dependencies and downstream effects
- engineering discipline in solving production-facing issues

## Transition
I used this engineering foundation to move into automation architecture and Quality Engineering, where I could build scalable validation approaches across UI, API, and database layers.

## Leadership value
This transition gave me a major advantage: I approached automation like an engineer, not just a script writer. It improved my framework decisions, defect triage ability, and collaboration with developers and architects.

## Outcome
My development background became a core strength in designing reliable automation frameworks and leading engineering-led quality transformation.
